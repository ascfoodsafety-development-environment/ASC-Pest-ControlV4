Kitchens & food areas

Fly & Ant Control in the Eastern Cape & Gauteng

Flies and ants are constant threats in kitchens, restaurants and food-processing areas both are contamination risks and a red flag in any food audit. ASC tackles flies with strategically placed insect light traps (EFK units) and source reduction, and ants with colony-level baiting that eliminates the nest rather than just the foragers you see. Combined with hygiene guidance, this keeps food areas clean, compliant and pest-free.

  • Insect light trap (EFK) placement & servicing
  • Fly source identification & reduction
  • Colony-level ant baiting
  • Drain & waste-area hygiene guidance
  • Entry-point proofing for doors & windows
  • Food-safe methods for kitchens

Why clients choose ASC for fly & ant control

A structured, documented approach built for South African homes, businesses and regulated sites.

Signs of a problem

Flies around bins and food prep, maggots in waste areas, ant trails along walls and clustered ants near sweet or greasy residues.

Why source matters

Killing visible insects without removing breeding and food sources guarantees they'll return.

Audit risk

Visible flies or ants in a food audit are an immediate non-conformance proactive control protects your grade.

How it works

Our fly & ant control process

Every programme follows a clear, repeatable sequence and every step is documented.

  • Assess. We identify fly species, breeding sources and ant trails to target the real source.
  • Install & bait. EFK units are positioned correctly and colony baits are placed along ant trails.
  • Reduce sources. We advise on waste, drains and hygiene to remove what attracts them.
  • Service. Units are serviced and monitored to keep food areas compliant year-round.

FAQ

Fly & Ant Control common questions

Where should insect light traps be placed?

Away from entry-point sightlines, not directly over food prep, and where flies naturally gather — we position and service them correctly for compliance.

How do you stop ants long term?

Colony-level baiting eliminates the nest, not just the foragers, combined with proofing and hygiene to remove attractants.

Are the methods safe for kitchens?

Yes we use food-safe placements and baits suited to food-handling environments and document them for audits.

Do you service the units regularly?

Yes. EFK units need scheduled servicing (tube and board changes) to stay effective we include this in your programme.
