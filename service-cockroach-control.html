Cockroach Control in the Eastern Cape & Gauteng

Cockroaches are among the most persistent and health-significant pests in South African kitchens, restaurants and homes. German cockroaches in particular breed rapidly and hide deep in cracks, appliances and warm voids. ASC uses a precision gel-baiting and monitoring approach rather than blanket spraying placing food-grade bait exactly where cockroaches harbour, tracking activity with monitors, and sealing the gaps that let them in. The result is faster knockdown, fewer chemicals in food areas and lasting control.

  • Species identification (German, American, Oriental)
  • Targeted gel bait in cracks, crevices & harbourage
  • Monitoring stations to verify knockdown
  • Crack-and-crevice proofing recommendations
  • Follow-up verification visit
  • Food-safe, low-odour methods for kitchens

A structured, documented approach built for South African homes, businesses and regulated sites.

Signs of cockroaches

Live insects at night, a musty odour, dark droppings like ground pepper, smear marks and egg cases (oothecae) in cupboards.

Health risks

Cockroaches contaminate surfaces and food, spread bacteria such as Salmonella, and trigger asthma and allergies.

Where they hide

Behind fridges and stoves, under sinks, in drains, motor housings and warm electrical voids.

How it works

Our cockroach control process

Every programme follows a clear, repeatable sequence and every step is documented.

  • Inspect & identify. We confirm the species and locate harbourage in voids, appliances and drains.
  • Bait & monitor. Food-grade gel bait is applied at harbourage points and monitors track the decline.
  • Proof. We seal cracks, gaps and entry points that sustain infestations.
  • Verify. A follow-up visit confirms elimination and adjusts the programme if needed.

FAQ

Cockroach Control common questions

How quickly does gel baiting work?

Activity usually drops within days, with full control over two to three weeks as the bait disrupts the breeding cycle. Heavy infestations may need a follow-up.

Is the treatment safe around food and children?

Yes. Gel bait is placed discreetly in cracks and voids, not on open surfaces, and is far lower-risk than blanket spraying in food areas.

Why do cockroaches keep coming back?

Without proofing and sanitation, new cockroaches re-enter. Our programme combines baiting with exclusion and housekeeping advice to stop the cycle.

Do you treat restaurants and food businesses?

Yes we use food-safe methods and provide the documentation HACCP and BRCGS audits require.
